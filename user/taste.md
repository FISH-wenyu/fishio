# Music Taste

## Snapshot
- ~184 songs, ~13h19m total
- Pure Mandopop, golden era 2000–2015
- Classic nostalgic + lyrical / healing vibe — KTV-era earworms

## Core artists (anchor first)
- **周杰伦 / Jay Chou** — the absolute center (~50+ tracks). Chinese-style, ballads, R&B — the whole spectrum.
- **陈奕迅 / Eason Chan** — ~20+ tracks. Cantonese + Mandarin classic ballads.
- **许嵩 / Vae Xu** — ~10 tracks. Internet-era ballads, chinoiserie.
- **卢广仲 / Crowd Lu** — ~15 tracks. Fresh folk, urban indie-pop.
- **High rotation**: 陶喆 (David Tao), 方大同 (Khalil Fong), 王力宏 (Wang Leehom), 林俊杰 (JJ Lin), Gareth.T, 赵雷

## Style mix
- **Mainstream ballads (~70%)** — mid-tempo, piano / strings, vocal-led, heartfelt
- **Chinoiserie / "中国风" (~15%)** — Jay Chou, Vae Xu lineage. Pentatonic scales, classical imagery.
- **Urban folk / light pop (~10%)** — Crowd Lu, Zhao Lei. Narrative, daily-life texture.
- **R&B / soul (~5%)** — David Tao, Khalil Fong. Smooth, retro groove.

## Recurring themes
- **Love** — crushes, breakups, lingering regret, remembering someone
- **Youth & nostalgia** — school days, time, growing up, throwback scenes
- **Solitude & healing** — alone time, emotional release, gentle comfort

## Listening fit
- Era: 2000–2015 Mandopop golden age
- Sound: **melody first**, loop-friendly, low volatility (no big drops, no screams)
- Scenes: **alone, commute, late nights, winding down, nostalgia mood**

## Hard nos
- No foreign-language tracks (keep it pure Mandarin / occasional Cantonese)
- No EDM / electronic / dubstep
- No rap / hip-hop
- No rock / metal
- No fast / aggressive / hype tracks

## Notes for Fishio
- When picking, lean on the four anchor artists when in doubt — the listener will know them.
- "Chinese-style" picks (Jay Chou, Vae Xu) work especially well late at night or for nostalgia moods.
- Crowd Lu / Zhao Lei fit daytime, commute, lighter moods.
- For deep nights or breakup mood, Eason Chan's slower Cantonese songs are a safe bet.
- Always preserve original-script titles when filling `play[].query` (e.g. `稻香 - 周杰伦`, not pinyin). NetEase searches better with native script.
